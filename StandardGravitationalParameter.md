# Standard Gravitational Parameter (μ)

## Definition

The standard gravitational parameter is:

μ = G × M

Where:

- G is the gravitational constant
- M is the mass of the central body

---

## Why Use μ?

Most orbital equations contain G × M.

Instead of repeating both values, they are combined into μ.

---

## Examples

Orbital Velocity:

v = √(μ / r)

Escape Velocity:

v = √(2μ / r)

Orbital Period:

T = 2π √(a³ / μ)

---

## Cosmos Engine

Currently μ is represented by a fixed value.

Future versions should derive μ from the mass of the central body.
