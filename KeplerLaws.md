# Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

## Overview

Johannes Kepler discovered three laws that describe how planets move around a central body.

These laws remain fundamental to orbital mechanics.

---

# First Law

## Law of Ellipses

Planets move in elliptical orbits.

The central body is located at one focus of the ellipse.

Implications:

- Orbits are not perfect circles.
- Distance from the central body changes over time.

---

# Second Law

## Law of Equal Areas

A line connecting the orbiting body and the central body sweeps equal areas in equal times.

Implications:

- Spacecraft move faster near periapsis.
- Spacecraft move slower near apoapsis.

This law explains changing orbital velocity.

---

# Third Law

## Harmonic Law

T² ∝ a³

Where:

- T = orbital period
- a = semi-major axis

Implications:

- Larger orbits require longer orbital periods.
- Useful for mission planning.

---

## Importance

Kepler's laws form the foundation of:

- Orbital prediction
- Space mission planning
- Satellite operations
- Interplanetary transfers
