# What Is a Simulation?

---

## Goal

The goal of this chapter is to understand the concept of simulation and its role in science, engineering, and Cosmos Engine.

By the end of this chapter, the reader should understand what a simulation is, why simulations are used, and how they differ from reality.

---

## Motivation

Humanity has always sought to understand the universe.

However, direct experimentation is often impossible.

We cannot create new solar systems, modify the speed of light, or wait millions of years to observe the evolution of a galaxy.

Simulation allows us to study such systems inside a controlled computational environment.

---

## The Question

What is a simulation?

Is a simulation the same as reality?

If not, what distinguishes the two?

---

## Intuition

Imagine dropping a ball.

One approach is to perform the real experiment.

Another is to implement the laws of motion inside a computer and calculate what would happen.

The computer does not contain the real ball.

Instead, it reproduces the ball's behavior using a mathematical and physical model.

That process is called simulation.

---

## Explanation

A simulation is the process of creating a model of a real system and executing that model to predict or study the system's behavior.

A simulation does not contain reality itself.

Instead, it contains a representation of reality based on assumptions, physical laws, and mathematical approximations.

Therefore, no simulation is ever identical to reality.

---

## Scientific View

Simulation is one of the most powerful tools in modern science.

It is widely used in fields such as:

- Physics
- Astronomy
- Aerospace Engineering
- Biology
- Medicine
- Climate Science
- Economics

Simulations allow scientists to test hypotheses before performing real experiments or to study systems that are inaccessible, dangerous, or prohibitively expensive.

---

## Mathematics

Nearly every scientific simulation is built upon mathematics.

For example, when simulating motion, mathematical equations describe the relationships between position, velocity, acceleration, and time.

Computers solve these equations numerically and use the results to reproduce the behavior of the system.

---

## Cosmos Engine

Cosmos Engine is a physics simulation engine.

Its purpose is not to recreate reality itself, but to construct a computational model capable of reproducing the behavior of physical systems according to established scientific theories.

In the future, the same architecture will support experimentation with alternative physical laws and hypothetical universes.

---

## Summary

A simulation is not reality.

A simulation is a model of reality.

The more accurate the scientific model, the more accurate the simulation becomes.

Cosmos Engine begins by modeling our current understanding of physics and later evolves into a platform for exploration and experimentation.
