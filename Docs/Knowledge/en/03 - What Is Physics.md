# What Is Physics?

---

## Goal

The goal of this chapter is to understand what physics is and why it serves as the foundation of Cosmos Engine.

By the end of this chapter, the reader should understand the purpose of physics and how it helps us describe and predict the behavior of the universe.

---

## Motivation

Every day we observe countless phenomena.

Objects fall toward the Earth, planets orbit the Sun, light travels at a constant speed, and galaxies move away from one another.

The most important question is not simply *"What happened?"*

The deeper question is:

**"Why did it happen?"**

Physics exists to answer that question.

---

## The Question

What is physics?

Is physics merely a collection of equations, or is it a systematic way of understanding reality?

---

## Intuition

Imagine observing that every object you release falls toward the ground.

Eventually you notice that this behavior is consistent.

Naturally, questions arise:

- Is this always true?
- Is there a rule behind it?
- Can this behavior be predicted?

Physics begins with questions like these.

---

## Explanation

Physics is the branch of science that seeks to describe the behavior of the universe through observation, experimentation, mathematics, and modeling.

Its purpose is not simply to memorize equations.

Its purpose is to discover patterns and laws that govern nature.

Once those patterns are understood, future behavior can be predicted.

---

## Scientific View

Physics is built upon four fundamental pillars:

- Observation
- Hypothesis
- Experimentation
- Modeling and Prediction

A scientific model is accepted only as long as it successfully predicts observations and experimental results.

Scientific theories are never considered absolute truth; they are the best descriptions currently supported by evidence.

---

## Mathematics

Mathematics is the language of physics.

Physical laws are expressed mathematically.

For example:

- Velocity relates distance and time.
- Force relates mass and acceleration.
- Energy is described through mathematical relationships.

Without mathematics, physics could not make precise predictions.

---

## Cosmos Engine

Cosmos Engine is built upon physical laws.

Every major component of the engine represents one or more physical concepts.

Examples include:

- Motion
- Gravity
- Orbits
- Energy
- Momentum

In this project, every concept follows the same learning path:

Understand the physics.

Express it mathematically.

Implement it in software.

---

## Summary

Physics is the scientific study of how the universe behaves.

It combines observation, experimentation, modeling, and mathematics to discover and describe the laws of nature.

Cosmos Engine follows the same philosophy by transforming scientific understanding into computational models.
