# 09 — Space

---

# Title

Space

---

# Goal

To understand space as the framework in which physical objects, vectors, and events exist, and to recognize its role in Cosmos Engine.

---

# Motivation

In the previous chapter, we introduced coordinate systems.

However, a coordinate system is only a method of describing location.

A deeper question remains:

Where do those locations actually exist?

Planets, stars, and spacecraft all move within something.

That "something" is what we call space.

---

# The Question

What is space?

Is space a physical object?

Is it merely a mathematical concept or a physical reality?

Why is the concept of space necessary?

---

# Intuition

Imagine describing the location of a book inside your room.

You might say:

- Two meters from the west wall.
- One meter above the floor.
- Three meters from the window.

These measurements make sense only because the room exists.

Space plays the same role for the universe.

Objects exist and move within space.

---

# Explanation

In its simplest definition, space is the framework in which physical objects exist and move.

Space allows us to:

- Describe positions.
- Measure distances.
- Define directions.
- Analyze motion.

In classical mechanics, space is modeled as three-dimensional.

---

# Scientific View

Classical physics models space as a continuous three-dimensional structure.

Its dimensions are:

- Length (X)
- Width (Y)
- Height (Z)

Every point can therefore be represented by three coordinates.

Modern physics, particularly Einstein's theory of relativity, combines space and time into a single concept known as space-time.

At the current stage of Cosmos Engine, we use the classical three-dimensional model.

---

# Mathematics

A point in three-dimensional space is represented as:

```
P = (x, y, z)
```

The distance between two points is

```
d = √((x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)² + (z₂-z₁)²)
```

This equation is fundamental to many simulation calculations.

---

# Cosmos Engine

In Cosmos Engine, the simulated universe exists within three-dimensional space.

Every celestial body has a position.

Examples include:

- The Sun
- The Earth
- The Moon
- Spacecraft

Each stores its position using a Position vector.

Distance, direction, velocity, and gravitational calculations all rely on this three-dimensional model.

---

# Common Misconceptions

### 1. Space simply means outer space.

Incorrect.

In physics, space is a fundamental concept, not merely the region between stars.

---

### 2. Space is the same as a vacuum.

Incorrect.

A vacuum may exist within space, but the two concepts are not identical.

---

### 3. Space is two-dimensional.

Incorrect.

Classical mechanics models space as three-dimensional.

---

### 4. Space cannot exist without objects.

This depends on the physical model.

Classical mechanics treats space as existing independently of matter, while relativity describes a deeper relationship between space, time, and matter.

---

# Summary

Space is the framework in which all physical objects and events exist.

Understanding space is essential for describing position, distance, direction, and motion.

Cosmos Engine currently adopts the classical three-dimensional model of space.
